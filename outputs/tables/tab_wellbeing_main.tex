\footnotesize
\setlength{\itemsep}{2pt}
\item \textit{Notes}: Column (1) reports the control-group mean with the
  standard deviation in brackets. Columns (2) and (3) report ITT
  coefficients from the interaction regression
  $y_{ic} = \alpha + b_1\,\text{Treat}_{ic} + b_2\,\text{HighWTP}_{i}
  + b_3\,(\text{Treat}\times\text{HighWTP})_{ic} + \boldsymbol{\delta}'\mathbf{X}_{ic}
  + \varepsilon_{ic}$,
  where column (2) is $\hat{b}_1$ (low-WTP ITT) and column (3) is
  $\hat{b}_1 + \hat{b}_3$ (high-WTP ITT); standard errors computed by
  the delta method. Column (4) reports $\hat{b}_3$ with its standard
  error in parentheses; column (5) reports the Benjamini--Hochberg
  FDR-adjusted $q$-value for column (4).
  \text{Treat} $=$ \texttt{treated\_all} (any subsidy arm).
  \text{HighWTP} $=$ 1 if stated WTP above sample median (43.6\,\%
  of sample); stated WTP defined as \texttt{WTP\_amt}\,$\times$\,
  \texttt{WTP\_r1}. D2 uses pooled R1\,+\,R2 data ($N = 4{,}157$);
  D6 and E1 use R2 only ($N = 2{,}047$--$2{,}057$).
  Controls: county, market proximity, transformer timing, baseline
  connection rate, population, gender, age, housing quality, baseline
  energy spending. Standard errors clustered at the community level.
  Asterisks indicate significance (two-tailed):
  $^{*}p<0.10$;\;$^{**}p<0.05$;\;$^{***}p<0.01$.
\item[$\dagger$] D6 is exploratory. It does not survive
  Benjamini--Hochberg correction ($q = 0.950$) and is available in
  R2 only (\texttt{r\_crime\_index}).
\item[$\ddagger$] E1 is the mean of the standardised D2 and D6
  z-scores. FDR $q$-value not reported for Panel E following
  Lee, Miguel \& Wolfram (2019).
\end{tablenotes}
